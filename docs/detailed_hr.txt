SECTION 1: EMPLOYMENT & ONBOARDING
================================================================================

1.1 EMPLOYMENT TYPES
--------------------
The Cary Company employs full-time, part-time, and contract employees across
nine warehouse and office locations in the United States. Full-time employees
are defined as those working 40 hours per week or 80 hours per biweekly pay
period. Part-time employees work fewer than 30 hours per week and are not
eligible for most benefits unless otherwise specified.

1.2 ONBOARDING PROCESS
-----------------------
New employees are required to complete onboarding within their first 30 days.
Onboarding includes completion of all federal and state tax withholding forms
(W-4, state equivalents), I-9 employment eligibility verification, direct
deposit enrollment, benefits enrollment, safety training certification, and
IT systems access setup including Microsoft 365, SharePoint, and internal
platforms.

New hires are assigned an onboarding buddy from their department for the first
60 days. The onboarding buddy is responsible for answering day-to-day questions,
providing department-specific guidance, and facilitating introductions to key
stakeholders.

1.3 PROBATIONARY PERIOD
------------------------
All new employees serve a 90-day probationary period beginning on their first
day of employment. During this period, employees are evaluated on performance,
attendance, cultural fit, and role-specific competencies. Benefits eligibility
begins on day 1 of employment. The probationary period does not affect benefits
eligibility. At the conclusion of the 90-day period, managers conduct a formal
review and determine whether to confirm employment.

1.4 BACKGROUND CHECKS
----------------------
All offers of employment are contingent upon successful completion of a
background check including criminal history, employment verification, and
education verification. Drug screening is required for all warehouse, logistics,
and operations roles. Driving record checks are required for roles that involve
operating company vehicles.

1.5 RELOCATION ASSISTANCE
--------------------------
Employees relocating more than 50 miles for a position at The Cary Company
are eligible for relocation assistance of up to $5,000 for individual
contributors and up to $10,000 for manager-level and above. Relocation
assistance is paid as a lump sum in the first paycheck and is subject to
a 12-month clawback provision if the employee voluntarily resigns within
one year of start date.

SECTION 2: COMPENSATION & PAYROLL
================================================================================

2.1 PAY SCHEDULE
-----------------
Employees are paid on a biweekly basis, every other Friday. The Cary Company
observes a two-week pay lag, meaning work performed in a given pay period is
paid two weeks following the close of that period. Direct deposit is available
and strongly encouraged. Paper checks are available upon request.

2.2 SALARY BANDS
-----------------
The Cary Company uses a five-tier compensation structure:

  Level 1 — Entry / Associate:         $40,000 — $60,000
  Level 2 — Mid-level / Specialist:    $60,000 — $85,000
  Level 3 — Senior / Lead:             $85,000 — $120,000
  Level 4 — Manager / Principal:       $110,000 — $160,000
  Level 5 — Director and Above:        $150,000 — $250,000+

Compensation is reviewed annually during the performance review cycle.
Market adjustments may be made outside the annual cycle at the discretion
of the VP of Human Resources.

2.3 OVERTIME
-------------
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x their regular
hourly rate for hours worked in excess of 40 hours per workweek, in
accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act. Overtime must be pre-approved
by the direct manager. Unauthorized overtime will be paid but may result
in disciplinary action.

2.4 SHIFT DIFFERENTIALS
------------------------
Warehouse and operations employees working evening shifts (3pm — 11pm)
receive a shift differential of $1.50 per hour. Night shift employees
(11pm — 7am) receive a differential of $2.50 per hour. Weekend shifts
receive a $1.00 per hour differential.

2.5 BONUS STRUCTURE
--------------------
The Cary Company offers an annual performance bonus for eligible employees.
Bonus eligibility begins after 90 days of employment. Target bonus amounts
by level:

  Level 1:  3% — 5% of base salary
  Level 2:  5% — 8% of base salary
  Level 3:  8% — 12% of base salary
  Level 4:  12% — 18% of base salary
  Level 5:  18% — 25% of base salary

Bonuses are paid in March following the close of the fiscal year (December 31).
Employees must be actively employed on the bonus payment date to receive the
bonus. Pro-rated bonuses are not available for employees who leave before
the payment date.

2.6 REFERRAL BONUS
-------------------
Employees who refer a candidate who is subsequently hired and completes 90
days of employment are eligible for a referral bonus. Standard referral
bonus is $1,000. Referral bonus for engineering, technical, and AI roles
is $2,500. Referral bonus is paid in the paycheck following the referred
employee's 90-day anniversary.

SECTION 3: TIME OFF & LEAVE POLICIES
================================================================================

3.1 PTO ACCRUAL BY TENURE
--------------------------
The Cary Company provides Paid Time Off (PTO) that combines vacation,
personal days, and floating holidays into a single bank. PTO accrues
on a biweekly basis based on years of continuous service:

  Years 0 — 3:     15 days per year (4.62 hours per biweekly period)
  Years 3 — 6:     18 days per year (5.54 hours per biweekly period)
  Years 6 — 10:    22 days per year (6.77 hours per biweekly period)
  Years 10+:       25 days per year (7.69 hours per biweekly period)

PTO begins accruing on the first day of employment. Employees may use
accrued PTO immediately — there is no waiting period. Maximum PTO carryover
is 40 hours (5 days) per calendar year. Unused PTO above 40 hours is
forfeited on January 1 each year. PTO is paid out upon termination at
the employee's current hourly rate for all accrued, unused hours.

3.2 PTO REQUEST PROCESS
------------------------
PTO requests must be submitted through the HR portal in Microsoft SharePoint
at least 5 business days in advance for requests of 3 or more consecutive
days. Single-day or two-day requests require 48 hours advance notice where
possible. Emergency PTO is available at manager discretion. PTO requests
are approved or denied by the direct manager within 2 business days.
Denial of PTO must include a documented business reason.

3.3 SICK LEAVE
---------------
The Cary Company provides separate sick leave in addition to PTO. Full-time
employees accrue sick leave at a rate of 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to
a maximum of 56 hours (7 days) per year. Sick leave does not carry over
year to year and is not paid out upon termination. Sick leave may be used
for the employee's own illness, injury, or medical appointment, or to care
for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or domestic partner).

For sick leave of 3 or more consecutive days, a doctor's note or medical
documentation is required upon return to work. Employees on sick leave
for more than 5 consecutive days should contact HR to discuss FMLA
eligibility.

3.4 9/80 WORK SCHEDULE
-----------------------
The Cary Company offers a 9/80 alternative work schedule for eligible
office-based employees. Under the 9/80 schedule, employees work 80 hours
over 9 days in a two-week pay period, with every other Friday off.

Week 1: Monday — Thursday 9 hours, Friday 8 hours (total: 44 hours)
Week 2: Monday — Thursday 9 hours, Friday off (total: 36 hours)

The 9/80 schedule is available to employees who have completed 90 days
of employment and have manager approval. Warehouse and operations roles
may not be eligible based on operational requirements. Employees on the
9/80 schedule who take a holiday that falls on their off Friday do not
receive an additional day off. The 9/80 schedule may be revoked if
performance or attendance issues arise.

3.5 REMOTE WORK POLICY
-----------------------
The Cary Company supports a hybrid work model for eligible roles. Employees
in office-based roles may work remotely up to 2 days per week after
completing their 90-day probationary period with manager approval.

Fully remote work arrangements are available for certain roles and require
VP-level approval. Remote employees are expected to maintain core working
hours of 9am — 3pm in their local time zone and must be reachable via
Microsoft Teams during those hours.

Warehouse, operations, and customer-facing roles are not eligible for
remote work due to the nature of the work. Remote work privileges may be
revoked for performance or attendance issues. Employees working remotely
are responsible for maintaining a secure and productive work environment.
The Cary Company provides a one-time home office stipend of $500 for
fully remote employees.

3.6 HOLIDAYS
-------------
The Cary Company observes the following paid holidays:

  New Year's Day                January 1
  Martin Luther King Jr. Day    Third Monday in January
  Memorial Day                  Last Monday in May
  Juneteenth                    June 19
  Independence Day              July 4
  Labor Day                     First Monday in September
  Thanksgiving Day              Fourth Thursday in November
  Day After Thanksgiving        Fourth Friday in November
  Christmas Eve                 December 24
  Christmas Day                 December 25

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on the preceding Friday.
When a holiday falls on a Sunday, it is observed on the following Monday.
Part-time employees receive pro-rated holiday pay based on their scheduled
hours.

3.7 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
----------------------
Employees are eligible for paid bereavement leave upon the death of an
immediate family member. Immediate family is defined as spouse or domestic
partner, child (biological, adopted, or step), parent, sibling, grandparent,
or grandchild.

  Immediate family (spouse, child, parent):   5 paid days
  Extended family (sibling, grandparent):     3 paid days
  Close friend or colleague:                  1 paid day at manager discretion

Bereavement leave may be extended with PTO or unpaid leave with manager
approval. Documentation such as an obituary or death certificate may be
requested.

3.8 JURY DUTY
--------------
Employees called for jury duty will receive their full regular pay for up
to 10 business days of jury service. For jury service extending beyond
10 days, the employee will receive the difference between their regular pay
and jury duty pay for up to an additional 20 days. Employees must provide
documentation of jury summons to HR within 3 business days of receipt.

3.9 PARENTAL LEAVE
-------------------
The Cary Company provides paid parental leave for the birth, adoption, or
foster placement of a child:

  Primary caregiver:    12 weeks paid at 100% of base salary
  Secondary caregiver:  4 weeks paid at 100% of base salary

Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the birth or placement
event. Employees must have completed 12 months of continuous employment
to be eligible. Parental leave runs concurrently with FMLA where applicable.

3.10 FMLA
----------
The Cary Company complies with the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA).
Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave
per year for qualifying reasons including serious health condition of the
employee or immediate family member, birth or adoption of a child, or
qualifying military exigency. Employees must have worked for The Cary
Company for at least 12 months and 1,250 hours in the preceding 12 months
to be eligible. Contact HR at least 30 days in advance for foreseeable
leave, or as soon as practicable for unforeseeable leave.

SECTION 4: BENEFITS
================================================================================

4.1 HEALTH INSURANCE
---------------------
The Cary Company offers medical, dental, and vision coverage through its
group health insurance plan. Benefits are effective on the first day of
employment. Open enrollment occurs annually in November for coverage
effective January 1 of the following year. Qualifying life events (marriage,
birth, divorce, loss of other coverage) allow for mid-year enrollment changes
within 30 days of the event.

Medical Plans Available:
  PPO Plan:         Employee premium $180/month, Family $520/month
  HDHP Plan:        Employee premium $95/month, Family $280/month
                    Paired with Health Savings Account (HSA)
  HMO Plan:         Employee premium $140/month, Family $410/month
                    (Available in Illinois, Indiana, and North Carolina only)

Dental:
  Employee premium: $18/month
  Family premium:   $52/month
  Annual maximum benefit: $2,000 per covered individual
  Orthodontia lifetime maximum: $1,500

Vision:
  Employee premium: $8/month
  Family premium:   $22/month
  Annual eye exam covered at 100%
  Frames or contact lens allowance: $200 per year

4.2 HEALTH SAVINGS ACCOUNT (HSA)
----------------------------------
Employees enrolled in the High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) are eligible
for a Health Savings Account. The Cary Company contributes to employee HSAs:

  Individual coverage:   $500/year company contribution
  Family coverage:       $1,000/year company contribution

Company HSA contributions are made in January. Employees may contribute
additional pre-tax dollars up to the IRS annual limit ($4,150 individual,
$8,300 family for 2026). HSA funds roll over year to year and are owned
by the employee permanently.

4.3 FLEXIBLE SPENDING ACCOUNT (FSA)
-------------------------------------
Employees enrolled in the PPO or HMO plans may contribute to a Healthcare
Flexible Spending Account. Annual contribution limit is $3,200 (2026 IRS
limit). FSA funds must be used by March 15 of the following plan year
(2.5 month grace period). Dependent Care FSA is also available with an
annual limit of $5,000 per household.

4.4 LIFE INSURANCE
-------------------
The Cary Company provides basic life insurance coverage at no cost to the
employee equal to 2x annual base salary, up to a maximum of $500,000.
Supplemental life insurance is available for purchase at group rates for
employee, spouse, and dependent children. Coverage amounts and premiums
are provided during open enrollment.

4.5 SHORT-TERM AND LONG-TERM DISABILITY
-----------------------------------------
Short-Term Disability (STD):
  Waiting period: 7 calendar days
  Benefit: 60% of base salary
  Duration: Up to 12 weeks
  Funded by The Cary Company at no cost to employee

Long-Term Disability (LTD):
  Elimination period: 90 days (coordinates with STD)
  Benefit: 60% of base salary
  Duration: To age 65 or recovery
  Funded by The Cary Company at no cost to employee

4.6 EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP)
---------------------------------------
All employees and their household members have access to the Employee
Assistance Program at no cost. EAP services include:
  - Up to 8 free counseling sessions per issue per year
  - Legal consultation (free 30-minute consultations)
  - Financial counseling and planning
  - Work-life balance resources
  - Crisis intervention hotline available 24/7

EAP is completely confidential. The Cary Company does not receive any
information about individual employee use of EAP services.

4.7 WELLNESS PROGRAM
---------------------
The Cary Company provides a wellness reimbursement of $600 per calendar
year for eligible wellness expenses including gym memberships, fitness
equipment, fitness apps, weight loss programs, smoking cessation programs,
and stress management courses. Reimbursement is processed through HR with
receipt submission. Wellness reimbursement is considered taxable income.

SECTION 5: RETIREMENT & FINANCIAL BENEFITS
================================================================================

5.1 401(K) PLAN
----------------
The Cary Company offers a 401(k) retirement savings plan administered
through Fidelity Investments. Employees are eligible to participate
beginning on the first day of employment.

Employee Contributions:
  Employees may contribute up to the IRS annual limit ($23,500 for 2026,
  $31,000 for employees age 50 and over with catch-up contributions).
  Contributions can be made as traditional pre-tax or Roth after-tax.
  Contribution percentage can be changed at any time through the Fidelity
  NetBenefits portal.

Company Match:
  The Cary Company matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of
  base salary. The match is deposited per pay period concurrent with
  employee contributions. There is no waiting period for the company match.

  Example: Employee earning $80,000 contributing 4% ($3,200/year) receives
  a full company match of $3,200/year for total annual contribution of $6,400.

  Employees contributing less than 4% receive a proportional match.
  Employees contributing more than 4% do not receive additional match above
  the 4% threshold.

Vesting Schedule for Company Match:
  The company match is subject to a graded vesting schedule:

  Year 1:   25% vested
  Year 2:   50% vested
  Year 3:   75% vested
  Year 4:   100% vested

  Employee contributions are always 100% vested immediately.

Auto-Enrollment:
  New employees are automatically enrolled in the 401(k) at a 3%
  contribution rate starting on their first day of employment. Employees
  may opt out or change their contribution rate at any time. Auto-escalation
  increases contributions by 1% annually up to a maximum of 10% unless
  the employee opts out of auto-escalation.

Investment Options:
  The plan offers over 25 investment options including target date funds,
  index funds, and actively managed funds. A managed account option is
  available through Fidelity for an additional fee.

5.2 PROFIT SHARING
-------------------
The Cary Company has offered a discretionary profit sharing contribution
to employee 401(k) accounts in years where the company meets profitability
targets. Profit sharing is determined by the Board of Directors and is
not guaranteed.

Profit sharing eligibility:
  - Must be employed on December 31 of the plan year
  - Must have completed 1 year of service
  - Must have worked at least 1,000 hours during the plan year

Profit sharing contributions are allocated as a percentage of eligible
compensation. Historical profit sharing contributions have ranged from
2% to 6% of eligible compensation in years where targets were met.
Profit sharing contributions follow the same vesting schedule as the
company match.

5.3 EQUITY / STOCK OPTIONS
----------------------------
The Cary Company is a privately held company and does not offer publicly
traded stock. Phantom equity arrangements are available for Director-level
and above employees at the discretion of executive leadership. Details
of phantom equity arrangements are governed by individual agreements.

5.4 TUITION REIMBURSEMENT
--------------------------
The Cary Company supports employee professional development through
tuition reimbursement for job-related education.

Eligibility:
  - Must have completed 12 months of continuous employment
  - Degree or coursework must be relevant to current or future role
  - Must maintain a grade of B or better (Pass for pass/fail courses)
  - Prior written approval required before enrollment

Reimbursement Limits:
  Undergraduate coursework:   Up to $5,250 per calendar year (tax-free limit)
  Graduate coursework:        Up to $5,250 per calendar year (tax-free limit)
  Professional certifications: Up to $2,000 per calendar year

Reimbursement is processed after course completion with submission of
grade documentation and receipts. A 24-month clawback provision applies
if the employee voluntarily resigns after receiving tuition reimbursement.

5.5 PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT BUDGET
-------------------------------------
In addition to tuition reimbursement, all employees receive an annual
professional development budget:

  Level 1 — 2:   $500/year
  Level 3 — 4:   $1,500/year
  Level 5:        $3,000/year

Professional development budget covers conferences, workshops, online
courses, books, and professional association memberships. Budget must
be used by December 31 and does not carry over. Requires manager approval
and expense report submission.

SECTION 6: PERFORMANCE & CAREER DEVELOPMENT
================================================================================

6.1 PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE
------------------------------
The Cary Company conducts formal performance reviews on an annual basis.
The review cycle runs October through December for the preceding year,
with final ratings and compensation decisions communicated by January 15.

Review components:
  - Self-assessment completed by employee (due November 1)
  - Manager assessment completed by direct manager (due November 15)
  - Peer feedback collected from 3 — 5 colleagues (due November 15)
  - Calibration session across managers (December)
  - Final rating communicated to employee (January 15)

Performance ratings:
  Exceeds Expectations (EE):      Top 15% of performers
  Meets Expectations (ME):        Core performers, majority of employees
  Partially Meets (PM):           Below expectations, improvement needed
  Does Not Meet (DNM):            Significant performance gap

6.2 MERIT INCREASES
--------------------
Annual merit increases are tied to performance ratings and are effective
February 1 following the review cycle:

  Exceeds Expectations:      4% — 7% increase
  Meets Expectations:        2% — 4% increase
  Partially Meets:           0% — 1% increase
  Does Not Meet:             0%, may be placed on PIP

Merit increases are also constrained by position in salary band. Employees
at the top of their band may receive smaller increases or off-cycle equity
adjustments instead.

6.3 PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)
----------------------------------------
Employees receiving a Partially Meets or Does Not Meet rating, or those
with significant performance concerns identified outside the review cycle,
may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan. A PIP outlines specific
measurable goals, a 60 — 90 day timeline, and regular check-ins with the
manager. HR must be involved in the creation of any PIP. Successful
completion of a PIP returns the employee to good standing. Failure to
meet PIP goals may result in termination.

6.4 PROMOTIONAL PROCESS
------------------------
Promotions at The Cary Company follow a structured process:

Step 1 — Eligibility:
  Employee must have been in current role for minimum 18 months.
  Most recent performance rating must be Meets Expectations or above.
  No active disciplinary actions or PIP.

Step 2 — Manager Nomination:
  Direct manager submits a promotion nomination form to HR and their
  department VP. Nomination must include a business case, evidence of
  performance at the next level, and compensation recommendation.

Step 3 — Calibration:
  Promotions are reviewed in a cross-functional calibration session
  with department VPs and HR quarterly (March, June, September, December).

Step 4 — Approval:
  VP approval required for Level 1 → 2 and Level 2 → 3 promotions.
  VP and SVP approval required for Level 3 → 4 promotions.
  CEO approval required for Level 4 → 5 promotions.

Step 5 — Communication:
  Approved promotions are communicated to the employee by the manager
  with an updated offer letter reflecting new title and compensation.
  Promotional salary increases are typically 10% — 20% above current salary.

6.5 INTERNAL TRANSFER POLICY
------------------------------
Employees who wish to transfer to a different department or location may
apply for internal postings after completing 12 months in their current role.
Employees must notify their current manager before applying. The current
manager may not block a transfer but has the right to request a reasonable
transition period of up to 8 weeks.

SECTION 7: WORKPLACE POLICIES
================================================================================

7.1 CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------
All Cary Company employees are expected to conduct themselves with
integrity, respect, and professionalism. This includes honest communication,
respect for colleagues and customers, protection of company confidential
information, compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, and
avoidance of conflicts of interest.

7.2 ANTI-HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION
----------------------------------------
The Cary Company is committed to a workplace free of harassment and
discrimination. The company prohibits discrimination or harassment based
on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic
information, sexual orientation, gender identity, marital status, or
any other characteristic protected by law.

Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination should
report it to their manager, HR, or through the anonymous ethics hotline.
All reports are investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation
against employees who report in good faith is strictly prohibited.

7.3 DRUG AND ALCOHOL POLICY
-----------------------------
The Cary Company maintains a drug-free workplace. Use, possession,
distribution, or being under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs
during work hours, on company property, or in company vehicles is
prohibited and grounds for immediate termination. Pre-employment drug
testing is required for all positions. Random drug testing may be
conducted for safety-sensitive roles.

7.4 EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
--------------------------
Employees who incur business expenses must submit expense reports within
30 days of the expense. Expenses submitted more than 90 days after
incurrence may not be reimbursed.

Approval thresholds:
  Up to $500:       Manager approval
  $500 — $2,500:    Manager + Director approval
  $2,500 — $5,000:  Manager + VP approval
  Above $5,000:     Manager + VP + CFO approval

Travel expenses:
  Airfare must be economy class for domestic flights under 4 hours.
  Business class permitted for international flights or domestic flights
  over 6 hours with VP approval.
  Hotel expenses reimbursed up to $200/night domestic, $300/night
  international without prior VP approval.
  Meals: $75/day domestic, $100/day international (alcohol not reimbursable).
  Mileage reimbursement at the current IRS rate ($0.67/mile for 2026).

Expense reports are processed within 10 business days of approval.
Reimbursements are included in the next regular payroll cycle after processing.

7.5 TRAVEL POLICY
------------------
All business travel must be booked through the company's designated
travel management platform (Concur). Travel must be approved by the
manager in advance. International travel requires VP approval and must
be booked at least 21 days in advance where possible.

7.6 CONFIDENTIALITY AND NDA
-----------------------------
All employees are required to sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) as
a condition of employment. Confidential information includes but is not
limited to customer lists, pricing strategies, proprietary processes,
financial information, product development plans, and personnel matters.
Obligations under the NDA survive termination of employment.

7.7 INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
--------------------------
All work product created by employees within the scope of their employment
is the property of The Cary Company. This includes inventions, software,
designs, processes, and written works. Employees must promptly disclose
and assign any relevant intellectual property to the company.

7.8 SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
------------------------
Employees may use social media in a personal capacity but must not disclose
confidential company information, make statements that could be construed
as official company positions, or engage in conduct that violates the
code of conduct or anti-harassment policies. Employees should identify
their views as personal and not representative of The Cary Company.

SECTION 8: IT & EQUIPMENT POLICIES
================================================================================

8.1 EQUIPMENT POLICY
---------------------
The Cary Company provides employees with the technology required to perform
their job functions. Standard equipment includes a laptop computer (Mac or
PC based on role requirements), monitor, keyboard, mouse, and headset for
remote workers.

Equipment must be returned in good working condition upon termination.
Damage beyond normal wear and tear may result in deductions from final
paycheck as permitted by law. Personal use of company equipment for legal
personal activities is permitted within reasonable limits. Installation
of unauthorized software is prohibited.

8.2 ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
--------------------------
Company technology systems including computers, email, internet, and
communication tools are provided for business purposes. Limited personal
use is permitted. Prohibited uses include accessing illegal content,
gambling, excessive personal streaming, or any activity that violates
the code of conduct. Company systems are subject to monitoring.
Employees should have no expectation of privacy on company systems.

8.3 DATA SECURITY
-----------------
Employees are responsible for protecting company data. Requirements include:
  - Use of strong passwords (minimum 12 characters, complexity required)
  - Multi-factor authentication on all company systems
  - No storage of company data on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services
  - Immediate reporting of lost or stolen equipment to IT
  - No sharing of login credentials

8.4 AI TOOLS POLICY
--------------------
The use of AI tools including large language models (ChatGPT, Claude,
Copilot, etc.) for work tasks is permitted with the following restrictions:
  - Confidential company data, customer data, or proprietary information
    must not be entered into public AI tools
  - AI-generated work product must be reviewed and verified by the employee
    before submission or distribution
  - Use of company-approved AI tools (Microsoft Copilot integrated with
    Microsoft 365) is encouraged for productivity
  - Questions about approved AI tool usage should be directed to IT or
    the AI Integration team

SECTION 9: SEPARATION & OFFBOARDING
================================================================================

9.1 VOLUNTARY RESIGNATION
--------------------------
Employees who voluntarily resign are requested to provide notice in
accordance with their role level:

  Individual contributors (Level 1 — 2):   2 weeks notice
  Senior and Lead (Level 3):               3 weeks notice
  Manager and above (Level 4 — 5):         4 weeks notice

Notice should be submitted in writing to the direct manager and HR.
The Cary Company reserves the right to accept resignation immediately
and provide pay in lieu of notice.

9.2 INVOLUNTARY TERMINATION
-----------------------------
Termination for cause may occur immediately without notice for violations
including theft, fraud, workplace violence, harassment, drug or alcohol
violations, or gross misconduct. Termination without cause includes a
severance package based on tenure:

  Less than 1 year:    1 week severance
  1 — 3 years:         2 weeks severance per year of service
  3 — 5 years:         3 weeks severance per year of service
  5+ years:            4 weeks severance per year of service, capped at 26 weeks

Severance is contingent upon signing a separation agreement and general
release of claims.

9.3 FINAL PAYCHECK
-------------------
Final paychecks including all accrued, unused PTO are issued on the next
regular pay date following the last day of work, or sooner as required
by state law. Illinois law requires final pay by the next regular payday.

9.4 COBRA CONTINUATION COVERAGE
---------------------------------
Upon separation, employees and covered dependents are eligible for COBRA
continuation of health, dental, and vision coverage for up to 18 months
(36 months for certain qualifying events). COBRA election notice will be
mailed within 14 days of separation. COBRA premiums are paid entirely by
the former employee at the group rate plus a 2% administrative fee.

9.5 REFERENCE POLICY
---------------------
The Cary Company provides employment verification only — confirming dates
of employment, job title, and eligibility for rehire. HR will not provide
performance references. Manager references are at the individual manager's
discretion and must be disclosed as personal rather than official company
references.

may include a cost-of-living adjustment to compensation at HR's discretion.
Relocation assistance may be available for company-initiated transfers.
