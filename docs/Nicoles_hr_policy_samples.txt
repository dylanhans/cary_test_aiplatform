SECTION 1: TIME OFF AND SCHEDULING

PTO POLICY
Full-time employees accrue 15 days PTO annually in
years 1-3, increasing to 20 days in years 4+. PTO
requests require 2 weeks advance notice for periods
over 3 days. Unused PTO up to 5 days may roll over
annually. PTO requests are submitted through the HR
portal and require direct manager approval.

SICK LEAVE
Employees receive 5 paid sick days per calendar year.
Sick days do not roll over. For absences exceeding 3
consecutive days, a doctor's note is required. Sick
leave cannot be used for personal time or vacation.

BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
Employees receive up to 5 paid days for the loss of
an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent,
sibling). Up to 3 paid days for extended family
(grandparents, in-laws). Additional unpaid leave may
be requested with manager approval.

JURY DUTY
Full pay is provided for up to 10 business days of
jury duty. Employees must provide the jury summons
to HR within 3 days of receipt. Any jury compensation
received is retained by the employee.

9/80 SCHEDULE
Available after 1 year of employment. Employees work
80 hours over 9 days with every other Friday off.
Core hours are 8am-3pm CST on scheduled workdays.
Schedule requires department head and HR approval.
Must be reviewed annually.

REMOTE WORK POLICY
Hybrid arrangements are available following successful
completion of 90-day onboarding period. Remote days
require manager approval and must maintain core hours
of 9am-3pm CST. Employees are expected onsite for
team meetings and client visits. Remote work is a
privilege and may be revoked with 2 weeks notice if
performance or collaboration is impacted.

SECTION 2: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

PAYROLL SCHEDULE
Employees are paid bi-weekly on Fridays. Direct
deposit is mandatory for all employees. Pay stubs
are available through the employee portal. Payroll
cutoff is Tuesday at 5pm CST for the following
Friday pay date.

EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
Business expenses require itemized receipts and
manager approval within 30 days of the expense.
Standard IRS mileage rate applies for personal
vehicle use on company business. Travel exceeding
$500 requires pre-approval from department head.
Expense reports are submitted through Concur.

HEALTH INSURANCE
Medical, dental, and vision coverage begins on the
first day of the month following 30 days of
employment. The company covers 80% of employee
premiums and 50% of dependent premiums. Open
enrollment occurs each November for January 1
effective dates. Qualifying life events allow
enrollment changes within 30 days.

401K AND PROFIT SHARING
Employees are eligible for the 401k plan after 90
days of employment. The company matches 100% of
contributions up to 4% of salary. Vesting is
immediate for employee contributions and 3-year
graded vesting for company match. Profit sharing
distributions are made annually in Q1 based on
prior year company performance and individual
performance ratings.

SHORT TERM INCENTIVE PLAN
Eligible employees receive annual performance
bonuses based on individual and company goal
attainment. Target bonus percentages vary by role
and are communicated at time of hire. Bonuses are
paid in Q1 of the following year. Employees must be
actively employed on the payment date to receive
the bonus.

SECTION 3: WORKPLACE POLICIES

CODE OF CONDUCT
All employees are expected to treat colleagues,
customers, and vendors with respect and
professionalism. Harassment, discrimination, and
retaliation of any kind are strictly prohibited.
Violations should be reported to HR or through the
anonymous ethics hotline at 1-800-XXX-XXXX.

CONFIDENTIALITY
Employees must protect confidential company
information including customer data, pricing,
financial information, and strategic plans.
Confidentiality obligations continue after
employment ends. Violations may result in immediate
termination and legal action.

TECHNOLOGY AND DEVICE USE
Company devices are provided for business use.
Personal use is permitted in moderation but must
not interfere with work responsibilities. Employees
have no expectation of privacy on company devices
or networks. Installation of unauthorized software
is prohibited. All data on company devices is
company property.

SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
Employees may not represent themselves as speaking
on behalf of The Cary Company on personal social
media without prior approval from Marketing.
Confidential information must never be shared on
social media. Employees are encouraged to engage
positively with company social media content.

DRESS CODE
Business casual attire is standard in the office.
Clothing with offensive graphics or language is
prohibited. Customer-facing employees should dress
professionally. Safety gear including closed-toe
shoes is required in warehouse areas at all times.

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE AND DEVELOPMENT

PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
The Cary Company uses a strengths-based review
process with 2-4 structured one-on-one meetings
annually with your direct manager. These replace
traditional annual reviews and focus on two-way
dialogue about performance, development, and goals.
Ratings are on a 5-point scale and directly inform
annual compensation adjustments.

PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
Employees receive up to $1,500 annually for
approved professional development including
conferences, certifications, and courses.
Requests require manager approval and must be
submitted before the expense is incurred. Unused
development budget does not roll over.

PROMOTION PROCESS
Promotions are initiated by managers based on
sustained performance above role expectations.
HR reviews all promotion requests for equity and
budget alignment. Promotions are effective on the
first of the following month after approval.
Employees may not request their own promotion
directly but may discuss career aspirations in
their regular manager one-on-ones.

DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
Performance or conduct issues follow a progressive
discipline process: verbal warning, written warning,
performance improvement plan, and termination.
Serious violations including harassment, theft, or
safety violations may result in immediate
termination without prior warnings.

SECTION 5: ONBOARDING AND OFFBOARDING

NEW EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING
The first 90 days are considered the onboarding
period. New employees complete mandatory training
modules in the first two weeks covering safety,
compliance, and company systems. A designated
onboarding buddy is assigned for the first 30 days.
The 90-day check-in with HR is required for all
new employees.

OFFBOARDING PROCESS
Employees leaving the company must provide 2 weeks
notice for individual contributor roles and 4 weeks
for management roles. Final pay including accrued
PTO is issued on the next regular pay date after
the last day of work. Company property including
devices, badges, and vehicles must be returned on
the last day. Exit interviews are conducted by HR
and are optional but encouraged.

REHIRE POLICY
Former employees who left in good standing and were
not subject to disciplinary action are eligible for
rehire. Prior service does not count toward tenure
or PTO accrual upon rehire. Prior 401k vesting is
preserved. Rehire decisions require HR and
department head approval.

SECTION 6: SAFETY AND FACILITIES

WORKPLACE SAFETY
Safety is a core value at The Cary Company.
All employees must complete annual safety training.
Warehouse employees require additional forklift and
hazardous materials certifications. Safety incidents
must be reported to a supervisor immediately and
documented in the safety portal within 24 hours.
Near-misses should also be reported to help prevent
future incidents.

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
In the event of a fire or emergency, employees
should evacuate using the nearest marked exit and
assemble in the designated parking lot area.
Emergency exits must never be blocked. First aid
kits are located in the break room and warehouse
entrance. AEDs are located at the front reception
and warehouse office.

PARKING
Employee parking is available in the main lot.
Visitor parking is reserved in the front row and
must not be used by employees. Electric vehicle
charging stations are available on a first-come
first-served basis. The company is not responsible
for damage or theft from the parking lot.
